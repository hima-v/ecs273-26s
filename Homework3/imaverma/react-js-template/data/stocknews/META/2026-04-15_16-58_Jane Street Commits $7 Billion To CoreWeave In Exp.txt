Jane Street is leaning further into AI infrastructure, in a move that could reshape how trading firms approach compute at scale. The firm has agreed to take an additional $1 billion stake in CoreWeave (NASDAQ:CRWV), paying $109 per share for Class A stock, while outlining plans to spend roughly $6 billion on the company's technology offerings. As part of the agreement, Jane Street will gain access to Nvidia's upcoming Vera Rubin chips, expected to be released this year, deployed across multiple CoreWeave data centers, alongside tools that could support the development and deployment of its own AI software.

The deal arrives during a wave of large-scale commitments toward CoreWeave, marking its third multibillion-dollar agreement this month. Recently, Meta Platforms (NASDAQ:META) committed $21 billion, while Anthropic indicated plans to spend multiple billions on data center capacity. CoreWeave, part of the so-called neocloud group, has been positioning itself as a provider of high-performance cloud infrastructure for AI workloads, with customers including Microsoft, OpenAI, and Meta, all seeking to expand compute capacity to support increasingly complex AI models.
For CoreWeave, these agreements could support a broader shift in its business mix. The company has been working to diversify beyond its reliance on Microsoft, which accounted for roughly 70% of its revenue last year, while expanding its own software and tools offering. For Jane Street, the investment reflects a deeper push into AI-driven trading infrastructure, where greater access to compute could allow the firm to process more data and execute more trades. That aligns with a business that generated more than $24 billion in revenue in the first nine months of 2025, surpassing its previous full-year record of $20.5 billion set in 2024.
